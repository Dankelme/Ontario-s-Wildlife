Ontario's Wild Life

Ontario is home to an incredible variety of wildlife thanks to its vast forests, wetlands, lakes, and grasslands. The province's forests cover about 66% of its land area and provide habitat for animals such as moose, black bears, gray wolves, white-tailed deer, beavers, and Canada lynx. Ontario is also an important region for birds, including great blue herons, bald eagles, blue jays, owls, and many migratory waterfowl that travel through the Great Lakes. Its wetlands and waterways support frogs, turtles, salamanders, northern pike, walleye, and hundreds of other species. From the boreal forests of the north to the deciduous forests of the south, Ontario contains diverse ecosystems that make it one of Canada's richest provinces for wildlife.
